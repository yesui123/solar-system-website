Fun Facts

  • Fastest winds: Neptune’s winds can reach extremely high speeds.
  • Most moons: Jupiter and Saturn each have many moons discovered.
  • Hottest surface: Venus has a very high surface temperature due to its thick atmosphere.
  • Shortest year: Mercury orbits the Sun the fastest.

Why Pluto is not classified as a planet

Pluto is a dwarf planet. In 2006, the International Astronomical Union (IAU) defined the criteria for a planet, and Pluto does not meet one of these rules because it has not “cleared its neighborhood” of other objects. It is smaller than the other planets and is located in the Kuiper Belt beyond Neptune. Although Pluto orbits the Sun and has its own moons, it does not meet all the requirements to be classified as a planet.
